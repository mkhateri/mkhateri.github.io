About Me

I am a final-year PhD candidate in Medical Computer Vision at the University of Eastern Finland, specializing in computer vision, computational imaging, and biomedical image analysis. My doctoral thesis, “Computational Methods for Brain Ultrastructure Analysis: Simulation, Super-Resolution, and Segmentation,” develops physics-aware and learning-based methods for diffusion MRI simulation, isotropic reconstruction of large-scale electron microscopy data, and weakly supervised segmentation of brain ultrastructures. I completed a six-month research visit at Harvard Medical School and Boston Children's Hospital, where I worked on MRI super-resolution and multi-view ultrasound compounding for fetal brain analysis in collaboration with leading research groups. My research spans electron microscopy, MRI, ultrasound, and multimodal medical imaging, with broader interests in deep learning, weak supervision, and data-efficient computational methods for clinically realistic deployment.
