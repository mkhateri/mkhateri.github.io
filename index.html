About Me

I am a final-year PhD candidate working on medical computer vision and computational imaging, with a focus on biomedia and medical imaging. My doctoral thesis, “Computational Methods for Brain Ultrastructure Analysis: Simulation, Super-Resolution, and Segmentation”, is in its final stages and is expected to be submitted by the end of February 2026.

During my PhD, I completed a six-month research visit at Harvard Medical School in Boston, USA, with ongoing close collaboration. My research specialization lies in computational imaging and medical computer vision, with experience across a broad range of biomedical and clinical imaging modalities, including electron microscopy (EM), magnetic resonance imaging (MRI), and ultrasound.

I enjoy building methods that connect imaging physics, deep learning, and downstream biomedical applications.
