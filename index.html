About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Medical Computer Vision at the University of Eastern Finland, working at the intersection of computer vision, computational imaging, and signal processing for biomedical and clinical applications. I recently completed a six-month research visit at Harvard Medical School, where I developed methods for MRI super-resolution and multi-view ultrasound compounding. My research focuses on physics-aware and learning-based approaches for super-resolution, denoising, Monte Carlo diffusion MRI simulation, weakly supervised segmentation, and multi-sensor image fusion. I work across real-world scenarios—from fully supervised to limited-supervision and self-supervised settings—and I am increasingly interested in multimodal biomedical datasets and data-efficient learning for clinically deployable AI systems.
