About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Medical Computer Vision at the University of Eastern Finland, working at the intersection of computer vision, computational imaging, and signal processing for biomedical and clinical applications. I recently completed a six-month research visit at Harvard Medical School, where I developed MRI super-resolution and multi-view ultrasound compounding methods. My work focuses on physics-aware and learning-based approaches for super-resolution, denoising, Monte Carlo diffusion MRI simulation, weakly supervised segmentation, and multi-sensor image fusion. I am also interested in multimodal biomedical datasets and data-efficient learning for clinically deployable AI systems.
